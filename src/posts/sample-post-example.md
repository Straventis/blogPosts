It has been more than 50 years since the TV remote was invented, and most of us still cannot turn on the television without hunting under three couch cushions for it. Now add a streaming box, a soundbar, and a smart light strip that only syncs through its own app. That, in miniature, is the agentic home problem.

The promise sounds simple. A home that just knows what you need, lights that dim themselves, a thermostat that learns your schedule. The reality, once you actually live inside one of these systems, tends to look more like five separate remotes that refuse to talk to each other.

## The Walled Garden

This is where Apple deserves an honorable mention. Its ecosystem is genuinely polished, but that polish comes with a toll booth.
